Things To Do in Mongolia

Unforgettable Experiences in the Land of Blue Sky

Mongolia offers an incredible array of unique activities and experiences that can't be found anywhere else in the world. From riding horses across the vast steppe to spending nights in traditional ger tents with nomadic families, this fascinating country provides authentic adventures for every type of traveler. Explore our guide to the best things to do during your Mongolian journey.

Horseback Riding on the Mongolian Steppe

Mongolians have been skilled horsemen for thousands of years, and there's no better way to experience the country's vast landscapes than on horseback. Whether you're a beginner or experienced rider, this is an essential Mongolian experience.

  • Multi-day Horse Treks: Journey across diverse terrains from grasslands to mountains
  • Khentii Mountains: Ride through the birthplace of Genghis Khan
  • Horseback Archery: Try your hand at this traditional skill
  • Nomadic Horse Games: Witness or participate in traditional competitions
View Horse Riding Tours

Stay with Nomadic Families

Experience authentic nomadic life by staying with local families in their traditional ger homes. This immersive cultural experience provides insight into a way of life that has remained largely unchanged for centuries.

  • Daily Activities: Help with herding animals and learn traditional chores
  • Traditional Cuisine: Learn to prepare and enjoy authentic Mongolian food
  • Handicrafts: Try your hand at traditional crafts like felt-making
  • Evening Entertainment: Experience folk songs, music, and storytelling
View Cultural Tours

Explore the Gobi Desert

The legendary Gobi Desert offers some of Mongolia's most dramatic landscapes and unique experiences. From towering sand dunes to ancient paleontological sites, the Gobi is a must-visit destination.

  • Camel Riding: Trek across the desert on two-humped Bactrian camels
  • Khongoryn Els: Climb the "Singing Sands" dunes at sunset
  • Flaming Cliffs: Visit the famous dinosaur fossil site at Bayanzag
  • Desert Wildlife: Spot rare animals like wild camels and snow leopards
View Gobi Tours

Witness Eagle Hunting Traditions

In Western Mongolia's Altai Mountains, Kazakh eagle hunters maintain the ancient tradition of hunting with golden eagles. Witness this spectacular cultural practice that showcases the incredible bond between human and bird.

  • Eagle Hunter Homestay: Stay with a Kazakh eagle hunting family
  • Training Sessions: Watch morning training of these magnificent birds
  • Golden Eagle Festival: Attend the annual festival in October
  • Photography: Capture stunning portraits of hunters with their eagles
View Eagle Hunter Tours

Discover Lake Khövsgöl

Often called the "Blue Pearl of Mongolia," Lake Khövsgöl is one of Asia's largest freshwater lakes. Surrounded by pristine forests and mountains, it offers a range of activities for nature lovers.

  • Hiking: Explore scenic trails around the lake's shores
  • Kayaking: Paddle on the crystal-clear waters
  • Visit Tsaatan Reindeer Herders: Meet this unique indigenous community
  • Summer Festivals: Attend the Ice Festival (winter) or Naadam (summer)
View Khövsgöl Tours

Experience the Naadam Festival

Mongolia's most famous festival celebrates the "three manly games" - wrestling, horse racing, and archery. This centuries-old tradition offers a vibrant display of Mongolian culture, athleticism, and national pride.

  • Opening Ceremony: Witness the spectacular cultural performances
  • Wrestling Tournaments: Watch Mongolia's strongest compete
  • Horse Racing: See child jockeys race across the steppe
  • Local Naadam: Experience more authentic rural celebrations
View Naadam Festival Tours
