Mongolia's Top Attractions

Discover Mongolia's Natural and Cultural Wonders

Mongolia is home to some of the world's most unique natural wonders and cultural sites. From the vast Gobi Desert to ancient Buddhist monasteries, the country offers diverse attractions that showcase its rich heritage and pristine landscapes. Explore our guide to Mongolia's must-visit destinations for your next adventure.

Gorkhi-Terelj National Park

Located just 55 km from Ulaanbaatar, Gorkhi-Terelj National Park is one of Mongolia's most accessible natural attractions. Known for its stunning rock formations, pristine rivers, and lush meadows, it offers a perfect introduction to Mongolia's natural beauty.

  • Turtle Rock: A famous rock formation that resembles a turtle
  • Aryabal Meditation Temple: A Buddhist temple nestled in the mountains
  • Horseback Riding: Experience traditional Mongolian horseback riding through beautiful valleys
  • Ger Stay: Overnight in authentic nomadic gers for a cultural experience
View Terelj Tours

Khustai National Park

Khustai National Park is famous for its successful reintroduction of the Takhi (Przewalski's horse), the last truly wild horse species on Earth. The park is located about 100 km southwest of Ulaanbaatar and offers visitors a chance to see these magnificent animals in their natural habitat.

  • Wild Horse Viewing: Observe the endangered Przewalski's horses in their natural habitat
  • Wildlife: Spot red deer, gazelles, wolves, and numerous bird species
  • Steppe Landscape: Experience the beauty of Mongolia's rolling grasslands
  • Conservation Education: Learn about wildlife conservation efforts
View Khustai Tours

Gobi Desert

The Gobi Desert is one of the world's largest and most iconic deserts, covering much of southern Mongolia. Despite its harsh climate, the Gobi is home to diverse wildlife, stunning landscapes, and important paleontological sites.

  • Khongoryn Els: Magnificent singing sand dunes reaching up to 300 meters in height
  • Bayanzag (Flaming Cliffs): Famous dinosaur fossil site with dramatic red sandstone formations
  • Yolyn Am (Eagle Valley): A deep ice canyon that can contain ice year-round
  • Camel Riding: Experience traditional Bactrian camel rides across the desert
View Gobi Tours

Lake Khövsgöl

Lake Khövsgöl, often called the "Blue Pearl of Mongolia," is one of Asia's largest freshwater lakes. Located in northern Mongolia near the Russian border, this pristine lake is surrounded by mountains, forests, and meadows, making it a paradise for nature lovers.

  • Crystal-Clear Waters: Contains 1% of the world's freshwater reserves
  • Hiking Trails: Numerous hiking paths around the lake with stunning views
  • Tsaatan Reindeer Herders: Visit the unique indigenous community that herds reindeer
  • Boating and Kayaking: Experience the lake's beauty from the water
View Khövsgöl Tours

Altai Tavan Bogd National Park

Altai Tavan Bogd National Park in western Mongolia is home to the country's highest peaks, including Khüiten Peak (4,374m). This remote region offers dramatic landscapes, glaciers, alpine lakes, and is home to Kazakh eagle hunters.

  • Five Holy Peaks: Mongolia's highest mountains and stunning glaciers
  • Kazakh Eagle Hunting: Witness the ancient tradition of hunting with golden eagles
  • Spectacular Hiking: Challenging treks through pristine wilderness
  • Ancient Rock Art: Petroglyphs dating back thousands of years
View Western Tours

Gandantegchinlen Monastery

Located in Ulaanbaatar, Gandantegchinlen Monastery is one of Mongolia's most important Buddhist centers. Miraculously surviving the communist purges of the 1930s, this active monastery offers visitors a glimpse into Mongolia's spiritual traditions.

  • Janraisig Temple: Home to a 26-meter-tall gilded statue of Avalokiteśvara
  • Morning Ceremonies: Witness monks performing daily prayers and rituals
  • Buddhist Art: Marvel at intricate thangka paintings and Buddhist iconography
  • Cultural Significance: Learn about Buddhism's role in Mongolian society
