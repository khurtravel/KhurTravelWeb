Tsaatan Reindeer Herders Journey

Experience the unique lifestyle of Mongolia's Tsaatan people, one of the world's last reindeer herding communities living in the remote northern taiga forests.

9 Days
Northern Mongolia
Max 8 people

Tour Overview

This unique journey takes you to northern Mongolia's remote taiga forest to visit the Tsaatan (Dukha) people, one of the world's last remaining reindeer herding communities. Beginning in Ulaanbaatar, you'll travel to the pristine shores of Lake Khövsgöl before venturing deeper into the taiga to experience the traditional way of life of these indigenous people.

The Tsaatan people have maintained their ancient traditions and nomadic way of life, moving with their reindeer herds through the forests and mountains of northern Mongolia. Living in tepee-like structures called "ortz," they depend on their reindeer for transportation, milk, cheese, and occasionally meat.

This tour offers a rare opportunity to immerse yourself in their culture, learn about their shamanistic beliefs, and experience the daily routines of reindeer herding. You'll also enjoy the breathtaking landscapes of northern Mongolia, including the beautiful Lake Khövsgöl, often called the "Blue Pearl of Mongolia."

This journey is perfect for adventure travelers, cultural enthusiasts, and photographers looking to document one of the world's most unique indigenous cultures.

Tour Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival in Ulaanbaatar

Upon arrival at Chinggis Khaan International Airport, you'll be met by your guide and transferred to your hotel in the city center. After check-in and some rest, enjoy a welcome dinner featuring traditional Mongolian cuisine and a cultural performance of throat singing and traditional dance.

Accommodation: Hotel in Ulaanbaatar

Day 2: Ulaanbaatar to Murun

Morning flight to Murun, the capital of Khövsgöl province. Upon arrival, we'll drive to nearby Uushgiin Deer Stone complex, featuring ancient megaliths dating back to the Bronze Age. After lunch, continue driving through beautiful landscapes to reach our ger camp near Murun.

Accommodation: Ger Camp

Day 3: Murun to Lake Khövsgöl

After breakfast, we drive to Lake Khövsgöl, one of Asia's largest freshwater lakes. The journey takes us through stunning steppe landscapes and rolling hills. Upon arrival, we'll check in to our lakeside ger camp and have time to explore the lakeshore and enjoy the pristine beauty of this "Blue Pearl of Mongolia."

Accommodation: Ger Camp at Lake Khövsgöl

Day 4: Lake Khövsgöl to Tsagaan Nuur

We continue our journey north to Tsagaan Nuur (White Lake) village, the last settlement before entering the remote taiga region. The drive takes us through beautiful forest landscapes and offers chances to spot wildlife. We'll spend the night at a local family's home or in tents, preparing for our trek into the taiga.

Accommodation: Homestay or Tented Camp

Day 5-6: Tsaatan Reindeer Herders Camp

We begin our horse or reindeer riding journey into the taiga forest to reach the summer camps of the Tsaatan people. Depending on their location, this journey can take 4-6 hours. We'll spend two days living with a Tsaatan family, experiencing their daily activities including reindeer herding, milking, and learning about their traditional crafts, cuisine, and shamanistic beliefs. We'll have the opportunity to ride reindeer, help with daily chores, and participate in traditional activities.

Accommodation: Camping near Tsaatan Family

Day 7: Return to Tsagaan Nuur

We say goodbye to our Tsaatan hosts and make our way back to Tsagaan Nuur village. This gives us time to reflect on our experiences and enjoy the beautiful taiga landscape one more time.

Accommodation: Homestay or Tented Camp

Day 8: Return to Murun and Flight to Ulaanbaatar

We drive back to Murun, enjoying the landscapes along the way. From Murun, we'll catch an afternoon flight back to Ulaanbaatar. Evening at leisure to explore the city or do some last-minute shopping.

Accommodation: Hotel in Ulaanbaatar

Day 9: Departure

After breakfast, transfer to the airport for your departure flight. End of our services.
