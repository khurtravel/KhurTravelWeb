Where to go in Mongolia?

Mongolia is divided into different regions including Khangai, Gobi and Steppe, each with unique landscapes, including forests, mountainous areas, vast steppes, and the Gobi Desert, all rich in natural beauty.

Khangai

A forested taiga and mountainous region that spans the central, northern, and western parts of Mongolia.

Steppe

An expansive, endless prairie that stretches across the eastern part of the country.

Gobi

A desert zone covering much of the southeastern part of Mongolia, and it is the second largest desert after the Sahara.

Western Mongolia

Known for its breathtaking scenery and unique cultural experiences, Western Mongolia is an adventurer's paradise. With snow-capped peaks, crystal-clear lakes, and a blend of diverse ethnic groups, this region is a blend of natural beauty and ancient traditions. The provinces of Bayan-Ulgii, Khovd, Uvs, and Zavkhan make up this remarkable area.

Western Mongolia is home to the dramatic Altai Mountains, deep valleys, and pristine alpine lakes. This remote region offers some of the most spectacular natural scenery in Mongolia and is home to diverse ethnic groups including Kazakhs, Tuvans, and Khotons.

Western Mongolia Highlights

Mountain Treks

Hike through the snow-covered Altai Tavan Bogd, Tsambagarav, and Kharkhiraa-Turgen mountain ranges, renowned for their stunning landscapes.

Golden Eagle Hunting

Discover the fascinating art of eagle hunting practiced by the Kazakh people, a centuries-old tradition.

Scenic Lakes

Take in the beauty of pristine lakes such as Uvs, Khoton-Khurgan, and Uureg, perfect for peaceful walks and relaxation.

Cultural Encounters

Meet the diverse ethnic groups of the region, including Kazakhs and other nomadic communities.

Ancient Rock Art

Explore the ancient petroglyphs in Shiveet Uul and Baga Oigor, where traces of early human life are etched into the landscape.

Khuumii Singing

Experience the mesmerizing sound of Khuumii (throat singing), an iconic form of music native to Mongolia's highlands.

Central Mongolia

Central Mongolia represents the heart of the country, both geographically and culturally. This region is home to the ancient capital of Karakorum, endless rolling steppe landscapes, and the cultural center of Mongolia.

Visitors to Central Mongolia can explore Khustai National Park, home to the reintroduced Przewalski's horses, the stunning Orkhon Valley UNESCO World Heritage site, and numerous ancient monasteries. The region offers excellent opportunities for horseback riding, cultural experiences, and historical exploration.

Central Mongolia Highlights

Karakorum

Ancient capital of the Mongol Empire with historical significance

Orkhon Valley

UNESCO World Heritage site with nomadic culture and beautiful landscapes

Khustai National Park

Home to the wild Przewalski's horses and diverse steppe wildlife

Northern Mongolia

Northern Mongolia features pristine taiga forests, crystal-clear lakes, and magnificent mountain ranges. The crown jewel of this region is Lake Khuvsgul, often called the "Blue Pearl of Mongolia" and one of Asia's largest freshwater lakes.

This region is home to the Tsaatan reindeer herders, one of the world's last remaining reindeer-herding cultures. Northern Mongolia offers excellent hiking, kayaking, wildlife watching, and authentic cultural experiences in a pristine natural setting.

Tsaatan Reindeer Herders

Darkhad Valley

Remote valley with stunning landscapes and traditional cultures

Southern Mongolia (Gobi Desert)

The legendary Gobi Desert in southern Mongolia offers a dramatic landscape of sand dunes, ice canyons, dinosaur fossil sites, and unique desert flora and fauna. As one of the world's largest deserts, the Gobi presents a stark contrast to Mongolia's northern regions.

Visitors can explore the towering sand dunes at Khongoryn Els, the dramatic red cliffs of Bayanzag (the Flaming Cliffs), and the ice-filled Yolyn Am canyon. The region is also home to desert wildlife including wild Bactrian camels, Gobi bears, and numerous bird species.

Gobi Desert Highlights

Khongoryn Els

Mongolia's largest and most spectacular sand dunes

Bayanzag (Flaming Cliffs)

Famous dinosaur fossil site with stunning red sandstone formations

Yolyn Am

Ice canyon that maintains ice well into the summer months

Eastern Mongolia

Eastern Mongolia features vast grassland steppes that stretch to the horizon, the birthplace of Genghis Khan, and the gateway to the Manchurian plains. This less-visited region offers a glimpse into Mongolia's history and traditional steppe culture.

The Eastern Steppe is one of the world's largest intact grassland ecosystems and home to massive herds of Mongolian gazelle. Visitors can explore Genghis Khan's birthplace at Dadal, the vast Dornod Mongol Protected Area, and experience the authentic nomadic lifestyle away from more touristed regions.
